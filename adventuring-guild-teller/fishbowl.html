The Fish-Bowl

Build the town first — alone, in a jar, with dials. A Godot .NET prototype of pillar III's townee sim: no desk, no floor, no dungeon. The only play is watching the readouts, turning the knobs, and authoring the people.

Clockwork stage·Pressure fuel·Storylet drama

Your brief for this slice: spec the village fish-bowl as a playable prototype — Godot with .NET code, GDScript, and JSON data; town-life simulation plus the creation menus' first pass; no guild-floor gameplay, just data readouts and debug knobs; and treat it like research. The machinery was chosen by survey — six approaches, scored, composited — on the studies page (FBS.1–FBS.6). This page is the proposal itself, in the house manner: ten claims to correct by handle, a hand-cranked mock of the observatory, and the rulings the build waits on. The LLM-facing build contract lives repo-side at plans/PLAN-village-fishbowl.md.

Update — built 2026‑07‑15. The first release now lives in the repo at adventuring-guild-teller/fishbowl/: an engine-free C#/.NET core + headless CLI + xUnit suite under a Godot 4.6 observatory. Every ruling in “The asks” was adopted on its recommendation; milestones M0–M3 are gate-checked and M4 is in place — 22 tests green, the golden day reproduces its seven beats, and the observatory boots clean. The one open item is VFB.Q1 gossip-yield tuning (the soak sits near four distinct lines a night), which the live knobs exist to explore. The claims and asks below are kept as the record.

What v0 is

Four things, and pointedly nothing else:

  1. I · the jar

    A seeded town

    12 townees, 6 places, 2 of them adventurers. Clockwork days, pressure meters, storylet events — the CPS machinery.

  2. II · the readouts

    An observatory

    Roster, inspector, place board, chronicle with because-lists, and the dawn summary. Watching is the game.

  3. III · the knobs

    Debug dials

    Pressure rates, storylet rates, the actionability dial, an away-flag for adventurers, seed and snapshots.

  4. IV · the menus

    Creation, first pass

    Townee creator, place creator, town generator, storylet browser — the creator pillar's v0.

The desk stays shut and the floor stays empty on purpose — the bowl is the substrate (AGT.2), so it gets built and trusted first.

My read, in ten claims

Same protocol as the pitch, tags re-glossed for a proposal: given follows from the brief or a settled ruling, read is a design choice I'm making (argued in the studies), gap needs a fresh ruling from you. Handles are stable and append-only — correct by number.

  1. FB.1givenThe bowl builds first, and alone.

    v0 contains no desk hooks and no floor verbs. The prototype's product is trust in the substrate: proof the town generates tellable, explainable material before any gameplay leans on it. Play = watch, poke, author.

    if the first playable slice should smuggle in any desk or floor surface after all.

  2. FB.2readThe machinery is the CPS composite.

    Clockwork day-plans for the stage (FBS.1), slow pressures and directed regard for fuel (FBS.2/FBS.5 distilled), JSON-authored storylets for the drama (FBS.4), a hearsay-lite filter on the summarizer (FBS.6 thinned). Planners and ledger economies declined, with reasons on the record.

    if a different machinery — or a different composite — should carry the bowl.

  3. FB.3readSlot-granular inside, day-granular outside.

    A day is 48 half-hour slots; storylets check once per slot after movement. The player-facing product stays the dawn summary (AGT.3's async cadence); the observatory's scrub-and-play controls are debug affordances of the prototype, never a promise that the shipped game shows a live town.

    by ruling VFB.D1 — half-hour slots vs finer vs continuous minutes.

  4. FB.4givenDirected regard, from day one.

    Every dyad stores a directed score plus tags (creditor, smitten, stall-rival…), and each townee carries a reserved regard-toward-the-teller — so the floor's two-score acceptance (AGT.8) drops in later without a data migration. Every storylet files the predicate snapshot that let it fire: causes stay citable by construction (AGR.2).

    if regard should stay symmetric until the floor actually exists.

  5. FB.5givenNothing in the jar dies.

    No death (AGT.12) and no expedition resolution in v0 — the dungeon isn't built, so an adventurer's absence is a debug away-flag that still ripples the town (farewells, moping apprentices, empty corner seats). The flag is the expedition system's stand-in, not its design.

    if the stand-in undersells expedition ripples you want simulated now (returns queueing at the desk, gear left behind).

  6. FB.6givenSummaries are gossip; bios are the answer key.

    Dawn shows 5±2 lines picked by tellability, only from events the town's telephone game actually carried (a witness, then a carrier). Every line cites its chronicle entry; every stat lives on the townee's sheet in the inspector (AGT.10). The summary is the town talking — the log stays backstage.

    if dawn should ever surface numbers directly.

  7. FB.7readThe actionability dial is a knob, not a guess.

    Three authored stops — hearsay · gossip · report — render the same chronicle entry at three levels of usefulness. AGR.3 asked where the line sits; the observatory exists partly to let you turn the dial and feel the answer (VFB.Q5).

    if the three stops mislabel the range you want explored.

  8. FB.8gapBios accrete marks.

    Authored prose stays verbatim; the sim appends dated one-liners beneath it — "Shouted at over arrears at his own gate; slept badly, day 1." — the Wildermyth move the research page endorsed, and the AGT plan's open question about player-authored backstories, graduated into a mechanic. Ships behind a toggle either way.

    ratify or strike — this is the AGT.13 candidate; your ruling here settles it on the pitch too.

  9. FB.9readThe creation menus are the creator pillar's v0.

    Four menus: townee creator (identity, statlets, traits, day-plan, home/work, authored bio, starting regard), place creator, seeded town generator, and a read-only storylet browser with force-fire. Scope holds the AGR.5 line — paperdoll, statlet, quotable prose, never a scripter — and customization stays optional beyond anything required (AGT.9). Creations write JSON to the user folder; promoting one into tracked data is a deliberate manual step.

    if the first pass should include in-app storylet authoring (VFB.D3).

  10. FB.10readAn engine-free C# core under a GDScript face.

    Fishbowl.Core (pure .NET: model, tick engine, JSON, summarizer) with xUnit tests and a headless CLI for week-long soak runs; one C# bridge autoload as the only engine-touching code; GDScript panels for every readout and menu; all content in JSON. Deterministic by construction — named RNG streams and a visible day-hash. And the bridge tolerates float-shaped ints from day one: this repo already paid for the lesson that GDScript's JSON round-trip re-emits 4 as 4.0, and that tests must replay the engine's own serialization path.

    by ruling VFB.D2 if you'd rather keep all C# in-engine.

      What the observatory must prove (the research questions)
      • VFB.Q1 · yield — ≥ 4 distinct tellable events per night at 12 townees, a week straight, without repeating itself into wallpaper.
      • VFB.Q2 · explainability — any dawn line traced to its causes in two clicks: line → chronicle entry → because-list.
      • VFB.Q3 · authoring cost — a new townee in under three minutes by menu; a new storylet in under fifteen by JSON.
      • VFB.Q4 · determinism — same seed, same week, same hashes — editor and headless alike.
      • VFB.Q5 · the dial — the three stops read differently enough to pick a shipping default by feel (AGR.3, finally measured).

      The creation menus — first pass

      Wireframes of the four menus (the forms below are sketches, not live). Everything writes ordinary JSON; nothing scripts behavior — the AGR.5 line holds.

      Townee creator

      writes a townees.json entry → user://town/

      Statlets, traits, a paperdoll slot, and prose — the whole authoring surface (AGT.9, AGR.5). The sim does the rest.

      Place creator

      writes a places.json entry → user://town/

      Places are graph nodes with hours — no geometry, no pathfinding. Travel is slot arithmetic.

      Town generator

      seeded; guarantees the invariants

      Everyone gets a home, a work, and at least one feeling; the town always has a gossip-carrier. Generate, watch a week, keep or re-roll.

      Storylet browser

      read-only + force-fire · authoring stays JSON (VFB.D3)

      Force-firing is how you interrogate a rule without waiting for the town to arrange it naturally.

      The build, in brief

      Full contract — schemas, bridge surface, milestone gates — in plans/PLAN-village-fishbowl.md. The shape:

      • code

        Fishbowl.Core (C#)

        Engine-free: model, tick engine, storylets, summarizer, JSON, named RNG streams, day-hash. xUnit suite + a headless CLI that soaks 3 seeds × 7 days and reports yield stats.

      • script

        GDScript face

        One C# bridge autoload (JSON across the boundary, float-shaped ints tolerated); every panel and menu is a GDScript node script. No sim logic outside the core.

      • data

        JSON content

        townees · places · dayplans · traits · storylets · simconfig — plus the golden-day fixture. Creations land in user:// and promote by hand.

      • gates

        Milestones M0–M4

        Scaffold → clockwork → pressures → storylets + summary (accepted against the canned day above) → creation menus + soak instruments. Each gate checked in-engine via the capture harness, never by tests alone.

      The asks

      Rulings the build waits on — reply by handle, same as ever:

      1. VFB.D1Tick shape.

        48 half-hour slots (recommended) vs 96 quarter-hours vs continuous minutes. Sets schedule grain, scrub cost, and how day-plans feel to author.

      2. VFB.D2Core split.

        Engine-free Fishbowl.Core + CLI + xUnit (recommended — honest tests and week-long soaks without opening the editor) vs all C# in-engine.

      3. VFB.D3Storylet authoring surface.

        JSON files only in v0 (recommended — AGR.5 discipline) vs a minimal in-app editor.

      4. VFB.D4v0 town size.

        12 townees / 6 places / 2 adventurers (recommended — readouts stay readable, tuning stays tractable) vs 24+.

      5. FB.8Ratify the bio marks.

        The gap claim above — sim-appended one-liners under authored prose. A yes graduates it to AGT.13 on the pitch; a no leaves bios static and the toggle shipped off.

      Reply by handle ("FB.3 is wrong — minutes, not slots"). Corrections fold into this page the pitch's way: edited in place, superseded text struck, new claims appended as FB.11+. Rulings on VFB.D1–D4 land in the plan and unlock VFB.M0. The studies (FBS.*) stay append-only.
