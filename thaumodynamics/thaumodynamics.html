Thaumodynamics

A field theory of fire & lightning

“Magic is not a suspension of the laws of nature. It is two further laws — and the bill for both.”

§ 1 · Premise

The machine has a twin

Every schoolchild learns the baseline fields: the electric field E, the magnetic field B, and the slow bookkeeping of heat. And every schoolchild learns the lesson of pairs — electricity and magnetism looked like two subjects until they were shown to be one machine. A changing B writes E into space; a moving charge writes B; light is the two chasing each other forever.

Thaumodynamics begins with an uncomfortable measurement. In 1877, Haldis Vane — hunting what she believed was a thermometer error — found a second pair of fields threaded through the first: coupled to it weakly, sourced not by electric charge but by the ordered hum of living nerves. We call them the Hearth field H and the Loom field Λ, and their charge anima.

Their mathematics will look familiar. That is the point. A spell is not a violated law; it is a boundary condition. The caster does not command fire — the caster arranges the fields so that fire is what physics does next.

§ 2 · The Fields

Hearth and Loom

H — the Hearth field “the shape of warmth to come”

A vector field that diverges from anima charge exactly as E diverges from electric charge. Where H threads matter, heat flows along it (Exchange I) — converge it and warmth funnels in; invert it and warmth funnels out.

Reach falls off as 1/r². Water blunts it badly; damp air is armor against fire.

Λ — the Loom field “the memory of motion”

Solenoidal — no sources, no ends, only loops. It is wound by anima currents (a gesture is a current) and by a changing H. To a moving electric charge it is a phantom magnet; broken quickly, it writes a true E into the air (Exchange II).

Copper holds a winding for minutes. Iron drinks it. This one fact organizes half of practice.

§ 3 · The Sector Laws

Four laws, set in a familiar hand

The second sector obeys four laws, printed here deliberately in the arrangement Maxwell chose for the first. Substitute H→E, Λ→B, α→q, and you recover the electromagnetism you already know. Nature, it turns out, liked the design enough to build it twice.

Law I · Anima’s Well

∇·H=αεθ

Anima charge is the wellspring of the Hearth.

Law II · Closure of the Loom

∇·Λ=0

The Loom has no ends; it only loops.

Law III · The Weave

∇×H=−∂Λ∂t

A changing Loom re-weaves the Hearth around it.

Law IV · The Return

∇×Λ=μθJα+μθεθ∂H∂t

Anima in motion — the gesture — spins the Loom.

The murmur — magic has a speed limit

cθ=1√(μθεθ)≈2.99 × 10² m s⁻¹

Chase Laws III and IV around a loop and a wave falls out: coupled ripples of H and Λ, called murmur, travelling at 299 metres a second in clear air — slower than a shout. Practitioners see it at the edge of vision as a violet unsettling of the air.

The consequence rules duelling: you cannot outrun a bolt, but you can outstep the loom that aims it. The shimmer arrives first, and a schooled eye reads it.

§ 4 · The Exchange Laws

Where spells come from

Two sectors that kept to themselves would be a curiosity for instrument-makers. They do not keep to themselves. Two weak couplings — measured, universal, and mercifully linear — let a condition arranged in the second sector drive the first. All practical magic lives in the two equations below. Everything after this page is engineering.

Exchange I · The Thermic Coupling — fire

q=−k∇T+χTH

Fourier’s law of heat flow grows a second term: heat flows along the Hearth field as surely as it flows down a temperature gradient. Converge H on a point and the room’s own warmth funnels there — that is Kindle. Flip the sign and the point’s warmth funnels out — that is Frostlock. Fire and frost are one discipline; ask any scarred instructor.

Exchange II · The Ionic Coupling — lightning

∇×E=−∂B∂t−ξ∂Λ∂t

Faraday’s law grows a second term: a changing Loom induces a true electric field, exactly as a changing magnet does. Wind Λ slowly, break it fast, and megavolts snap into the air. Lightning is not summoned. It is induced.

Moving charge feels the Loom as a magnet that isn’t there: F=q(E+v×B+ξv×Λ)

§ 5 · Anima

The charge, and the bill

Anima (unit: the thaum, th) is carried by coherent living electrochemistry — the phase-locked hum of a nervous system. Everyone hums. Few are in tune: an untrained soul is bright but incoherent, its fields cancelling in a centimetre. Training is not a search for more charge. It is a search for phase — which is why every tradition, whatever it claims, teaches its novices to count their own pulse.

Anima obeys a continuity law with one cruel amendment — outside living tissue, it fades:

Law V · Persistence

∂α∂t+∇·Jα=−ατα

Anima neither lasts nor leaks: it fades. τα ≈ 11 s in free air.

The eleven-second rule. No spell coasts longer than a breath. There are no enchanted swords in this cosmology, no rings that smoulder in a tomb for an age — a charge left in an object is gone before its maker’s footsteps are. Artifacts exist, but they are lenses, never batteries: a copper torc shapes what a living caster pours through it, and shapes nothing at all on a shelf.

The bill. Both Exchanges conserve energy to the joule, and the ledger closes on the caster’s metabolism. Boiling a kettle by Kindle moves ≈84 kJ — twenty food-calories, a bite of biscuit. A duelling bolt delivers ≈20 kJ but costs nearer 400 through coupling losses. Casting is cardio; war-mages eat like rowers.

Overcast. Exchange I runs on whatever gradient stands nearest. When a room’s warmth is spent, the well does not politely close — it draws the next reservoir, which is the caster’s blood. Fire-casters do not burn out. They freeze, from the inside, mid-triumph.

§ 6 · Casting

A spell is a circuit you become

Nothing in the laws mentions wands, words, or waving. Yet every tradition converged on the same triad, because the laws reward it. A spell is a boundary-value problem: source, geometry, drive.

◆Gesture — writes the current

A moving well of anima is a current Jα, and by Law IV currents spin the Loom. The circling arm is a solenoid winding; the flat sweeping palm, a sheet current. Footwork is not flourish — it is topology.

◆Focus — sets the boundary

Matter bends the sector as glass bends light (see §8). A heartwood wand holds your charge a hand’s-breadth ahead of your skin; a copper torc stores a winding; an iron ring is a drain you wear by mistake exactly once. The focus is the circuit board.

◆Word — keeps the clock

No syllable touches the fields. Words pace the drive — casters phase-lock to their own pulse, and a chant is a metronome you can carry into fear. A gagged caster still casts. They simply rush, and rushing is how torcs end up humming and hands end up scarred.

§ 7 · Worked Protocols

Three spells, printed as field conditions

From the practical curriculum. Read each as an engineer reads a circuit: what condition is arranged in the second sector, what the Exchange laws then oblige the baseline fields to do, and how it fails.

PROTOCOL 01

Kindle

Exchange Istatic wellnovice

Field condition

Hold coherent α in the focus. Shape H as a well: diverging from the focus, converging on the tinder point. Heartwood wand, silver core; useful reach ≈3 m in dry air.

Baseline response

By Exchange I, q = χTH funnels ambient heat up the well. The point climbs ≈140 K/s in still 20 °C air; dry tinder crosses autoignition (≈505 K) in about two seconds.

Result

Smoulder, then flame. Nothing is created: the room grows imperceptibly cooler, and the ledger lands on your breakfast (§5). The flame, once lit, is ordinary fire and owes you nothing.

Failure modes

Wet fuel — the latent-heat sink eats the flux. Deep cold — a thin reservoir; Kindle famously fails on glaciers. Counter-casting — an opposed well sums to zero: superposition is the whole of counterspelling.

Inverse form — Frostlock

The same well, phase-inverted: heat funnels out. Conservation collects its due — the ejected energy re-radiates as murmur, so cold-work glows a faint violet. Cold light is a refund.

When the room’s gradient is spent, the field takes the next one: yours. A student who will not stop at the first shiver is casting with the heat of their own blood. — H.V., margin of the first edition

PROTOCOL 02

Stormneedle

Exchange IIExchange I (phase ①)two-phase · journeyman

Field condition — ① the Thread

A hairline H filament from muzzle-point to target pre-heats a channel of air; thermal ionization drops its breakdown field roughly tenfold. The thread is the aim.

Field condition — ② the Quench

Λ is wound into a copper torc over ≈2 s by the circling gesture — then coherence is dropped at once. Not spent: dropped. The winding collapses in ≈80 μs.

Baseline response

By Exchange II, Eind = −ξ ∂Λ/∂t spikes to ≈1.8 MV/m — under dry air’s 3 MV/m, far over the threaded channel’s 0.35. Breakdown follows the thread: ≈20 kJ arrives in 60 μs at ≈30 kA, thunderclap included.

Failure modes

Slow quench — a hot, humming torc and no bolt. Rain — ionization comes cheap but the thread wanders: bolts fork. Iron within arm’s reach — the winding drains before it breaks. Never duel in a smithy.

PROTOCOL 03

Loomshell

Exchange IIstanding field · journeyman

Field condition

Comb a standing Λ tangent to a shell about the body, and re-comb it against the eleven-second fade. Copper thread in the hems halves the upkeep; every academy robe is secretly a component.

Baseline response

Incoming charged matter — bolt leaders, plasma, ionized spray — feels ξv×Λ and gyrates. When its radius of curl is shorter than the shell is thick, the strike walks around you and exits still looking for ground.

Result

Lightning parts around the caster. So does flame-plasma, which is why a shell-carrier can stroll through a burning granary looking insufferable.

Failure modes

The two polar whorls — a sphere of tangents cannot be combed flat (Poincaré–Hopf grants every shell exactly two defects; duelists aim for the crown). Neutral matter — arrows, stones, rain, steel pass untouched. Your own mail — iron on the defender grounds the defender.

The shell turns lightning, not steel. The academy repeats it every term because the graveyard repeats it too. — H.V.

Wild reading — will-o’-wisp

Decaying peat holds trace anima long past τα’s usual mercy. Over bogs it rings as a murmur standing wave: a lantern nobody is holding, eleven seconds at a time.

Wild reading — ball lightning

A self-knotted plasmoid of Λ and its own ionized air — nature’s only known quench that fails to finish. Stable for seconds; approached by exactly one researcher per generation.

Wild reading — storm-shimmer

A thundercloud’s charge separation faintly winds Λ at the scale of the whole sky. Bolts come cheap in rain and Kindle comes dear — the weather takes sides.

Warning · Overcast

Stop at the first shiver. The well does not know where the room ends and you begin. Sweet tea, and a fire somebody else kindled.

Warning · Quench discipline

Never wind what you cannot break cleanly. A slow quench is a hot torc and a missed bolt; a fumbled one is a self-strike.

Warning · The smithy rule

No duelling near masses of iron — your winding drains into every anvil. This is also why formal duels are fought barefoot, on dry ash.

Warning · Ward hygiene

Allied shells superpose. Two proud wards make an interference lattice that guides the bolt to both. Stand apart, or share one.
