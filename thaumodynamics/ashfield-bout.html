A duel in seven exchanges · cinderwork against needlework

The Ashfield Bout

Being a full and honest account of the Shrovetide duel between Edda Halloway of the Kiln Circle and Casimir Dree of the Storm Guild — with the Board’s instruments and arithmetic kept in the margin, where they belong.

From the record of Marshal-Chronicler P. Ivory · to three points · eleven paces · no iron

→ turn with the arrows, the keys, or a swipe

The Accord · what the Board permits

No edges, no iron, no mercy asked

The court is a chalk ring of eleven paces on a floor of dry kiln-ash, swept and grounded by the marshals’ rods between exchanges — the rods are the marshals’ instruments, never the duelists’. No iron stands within thirty paces. Both fight barefoot, in wool coats brushed with witness-chalk, hems threaded with copper. A coat shapes your own weave; the Accord calls that fencing. A grounding bar unpicks your opponent’s; the Accord calls that sabotage.

  • The Seal · 1 pt Each duelist wears three tallow seals — crown of the shoulder, hip, heart. Melt one of your opponent’s (62 °C, thermoscope-verified) and the point is yours. Tallow does not argue.
  • The Fern · 1 pt Land a gauged arc on the witness-chalked coat and it prints a silver fern the whole gallery can read. The strike is its own receipt.
  • Forbidden Edges and points; blows of fist or heel; any instrument whose purpose is the disruption of the opponent’s fields. Counter-casting with your own fields is lawful — superposition is fencing. The bind of hands is lawful. The bout ends at three points, a yield, or the marshals’ shiver-stop.

The Duelists · rated to the same ring

The kiln walks in; the needle buys time

Edda Halloway, 34

Kiln Circle · Adept of the Second Ring · cinderwork (Exchange I) · 41–12

Fights close and patient. Her art is the well — heat funnelled along H into a seal, or pumped out of the air until fog stands up in the ring. Reach falls with the square of distance, so her whole game is the walk-in, the angle, the bind. She wears no shell; shells are the other tongue.

Instruments: heartwood wand, silver core; a contact palm-aperture. One line per wand — remember that.

Casimir Dree, 31

Storm Guild · Adept of the Second Ring · needlework (Exchange II) · 44–15

Fights long and exact. His art is the line — a hair of warmed air laid to a target, then a winding broken all at once so the court’s own physics deliver the arc. Two full seconds to wind; eleven to let it rot. His whole game is buying those seconds.

Instruments: Board-gauged torc (Λ 80); a wrist flicker-ring (Λ 15) — legal, and too weak to arc. Theater, the Guild calls it.

The old problem, in numbers

her scoring reach: seal hold ≈ 1.9 s at arm’s length (flux falls ∝ 1/r²) his scoring cycle: thread 0.3 s + wind 2.0 s, spend it or lose it (τα = 11 s) Every exchange is a race between her feet and his hands.

Exchange the First · the probe

Nineteen thousandths of a second, politely declined

They salute with open palms — no edges to present — and Dree gives ground at once along the chalk, hands low, right wrist already turning its small circles. You feel his winding before you see anything: the fine hairs lift, and the gallery hushes at the wrongness of it. Then the thread: a violet unsettling of the air, drawn from his torc-sight toward her shoulder like a ruled line.

Halloway watches it come. That is not bravado; that is arithmetic. The shimmer crosses the court slower than a thrown apple, and she has read wind-ups since she was a girl at the kiln-rows. One flat step off the line — ash barely disturbed — and the thread hangs in empty air, cooling, useless. Dree lets the winding rot rather than spend it badly. Eleven seconds later it is gone, and he has paid a meal for a look at her footwork.

“She did not dodge the bolt. There was never a bolt. She declined the aim, the way you decline a bad contract.” — the chronicler’s note

The Board’s margin — exchange I

range r = 5.8 m → shimmer transit r/cθ = 5.8 ÷ 299 = 19 ms wind-up to quench: 2.00 s, telegraphed from the first wrist-turn her dodge budget: react 0.25 + step 0.50 = 0.75 s margin: 2.00 − 0.02 − 0.75 = +1.23 s — a stroll unspent winding decays e−t/11s: his probe cost ≈ 45 kJ, returned nothing Verdict: at five paces the needle cannot score on an honest line. Both know it. The bout will be decided by dishonest ones.

Exchange the Second · the flicker

Two shimmers, one lie

The second exchange opens the same way — the wrist, the hush, a thread to her shoulder — and this time a quench cracks. Violet light pops off his wrist and the gallery flinches as one animal. Halloway is already moving; you do not stand your ground against a break. She takes the safe step, the schooled step, the one the manuals print: off-line and toward his open side.

Which is where the second thread has been lying since before the flash. The true quench takes her at the shoulder mid-stride — a crack like green wood, a smell of hot chalk — and when she straightens there is a silver fern printed collarbone to sleeve, delicate as frost on a window. The marshals’ wands go up. Dree inclines his head, all courtesy. The flash, you understand, could never have struck anyone. It was punctuation.

“The flicker-ring cannot arc. Every adept in the gallery knew it. Her feet knew the manuals instead.”

Score after: Halloway 0 — 1 Dree · fern, shoulder

The Board’s margin — exchange II

flicker-ring: E = 0.16×15 ÷ 3.0×10⁻⁵ = 0.080 MV/m < 0.35 — cannot arc, even threaded. Pure theater. thermoscope log: two threads on the court — the second laid 0.9 s before the flash. The instruments saw the feint before the gallery did. her commit: react 0.25 s, step 0.50 s → on the laid line at t + 0.62 s his true quench: aim 0.15 s after her plant · torc 0.427 MV/m down a made line → fern delivered ≈ 1.1 kJ — a welt and a lesson, per gauge Verdict: he did not beat her reflexes; he rented them. Point, Dree.

Exchange the Third · the scrub and the bind

She unpicks his line and walks down it

Now the bout shows its teeth. Dree threads again — and the thread dies. You can see the moment it dies: the shimmer dims mid-court like a lamp behind a door. Halloway has laid her own line along his, cold against warm, sum against sum, and what remains is ordinary air that no gauged torc in Candela can break. The Guild benches go very quiet. Counter-casting is fencing, says the Accord. This is fencing the way a locksmith fences.

And she is already walking — not rushing, walking, on the beat, because his hands are empty and hers are not. He gives ground; the chalk stops him; the ring is only so kind. She takes his wrist as he pivots, crosses it down, and lays her palm a hand’s-breadth off his hip. Two seconds is an eternity in a bind. The gallery counts them aloud, the brutes. The hip seal slumps, weeps, lets go. The thermoscope confirms what everyone smelled.

“Her counter-line cost less than his thread. That is the whole cruelty of the First Exchange: it is cheaper to refuse a fire than to light one.”

Score after: Halloway 1 — 1 Dree · seal, hip

The Board’s margin — exchange III

his thread: channel +240 K · her counter-line: −240 K · superposed sum ≈ 0 channel breakdown stays 3.0 MV/m ≫ 0.427 — the needle is refused, not resisted tempo: her walk 4.0 m ÷ 1.9 m/s = 2.1 s · his re-wind 2.0 + re-thread 0.3 = 2.3 s → she arrives 0.2 s early the seal: Q = 1.8 g × (2.1×40 + 190) ≈ 0.49 kJ · palm flux 260 W → 1.9 s held Verdict: the scrub costs her 8 kJ; his dead thread cost 12. Economics, then footwork, then wax. Point, Halloway.

Exchange the Fourth · the two threads

One wand cannot say no twice

Dree has learned the price of honest lines, so he stops selling them. First he sets his wrist-ring humming — not a quench, a flutter, a fast small trembling of the weave that you feel in your teeth at ten paces. Wherever her well tries to gather, the trembling combs it sideways; her heat arrives everywhere and nowhere, a kettle poured on a moving cart. Her reach, already short, gets shorter. She must come to him again. He is counting on it.

As she advances he shows her a thread at shoulder height — obvious, insulting, and she kills it with her one free line, precisely as before. The second thread is already lying along the ash like a snake in dust, ankle height, in the lane her own advance has chosen. The quench takes her at the coat-skirt. A second fern, low and wide. The gallery, which adored her a minute ago, adores him now. Galleries.

“A wand holds one line. A liar needs two. The arithmetic of deceit is not deep, but it is exact.”

Score after: Halloway 1 — 2 Dree · fern, coat-skirt

The Board’s margin — exchange IV

flutter: Λ 12 at 38 Hz → |∂Λ/∂t| ≈ 2πfΛ ≈ 2.9×10³ u/s — no arc, but by the Weave law it writes curl into H her well convergence at range: −35 % (Board est.) → seal-hold stretches 1.9 → 2.9 s: unviable on a moving man scrub capacity: one line per wand · threads on court: two low thread’s lane = her own chosen advance — the aim was her plan, not her position Verdict: he spent 110 kJ this exchange to make her art expensive and her honesty predictable. Point, Dree.

Exchange the Fifth · the fog

She refunds the court’s warmth, with interest

Down a point and shorter-armed by the minute, Halloway does the thing the Kiln Circle forbids its novices and drills into its adepts. She opens both hands, and pulls. Not at him — at the air between them, a wedge of it, wide as a cart. The warmth goes out of it the way sound goes out of a room. Fog stands up out of nothing, a grey wall shot through with that violet gleam the cold-work always wears — the refund, the Circle calls it — and the middle of the court simply ceases to exist for the eye.

Understand what she has bought. His art needs a line: a seen target, a dry channel, a clean aim through the torc. Hers needs an arm’s length and a heartbeat. In fog, damp air drinks her reach too — but she was never going to score at range again, and now neither is he. The gallery hears footsteps on ash, slow, circling. Someone laughs nervously and is shushed. The marshals lean in, thermoscopes up, watching two ghosts by instrument light.

“The fog cost her a third of her supper and half his art. She would tell you that is the best price in the ring.”

Score after: Halloway 1 — 2 Dree · no score — the court itself changed hands

The Board’s margin — exchange V

wedge ≈ 2.5 m³ · 21 °C, RH 55 % → dew point 11.6 °C Q = 2.5 × 1.2 × 1005 × 9.4 ≈ 28 kJ pumped out over 6.0 s (diffuse cast ≈ 4.7 kW — lawful; the gauge caps flux on the person) her ledger: 28 ÷ 0.21 ≈ 135 kJ ≈ 32 kcal — sprinter’s spending damp air: εθ up → her reach −40 % · his thread untenable (droplets drink the filament, ×4 cost, the line sags) fog breakdown ≈ 0.40 MV/m < 0.427 — note well: wild arcs are now possible, and unaimable Verdict: she has made the court honest again — nobody can lie about a line nobody can see. The Board’s instruments record a 0.9 K drop in her core. The shiver-stop threshold is 1.2.

Exchange the Sixth · the fork and the heart

He gambles on the weather he was given

Dree does what the desperate and the brilliant do: he uses her weather. Damp air breaks cheap — every stormhand knows it, every stormhand is warned. He winds full, aims at a footfall, and quenches into the fog. The court goes white. The arc forks like a river delta, two tines howling past her shoulder and down into the ash, and for one instant the fog is a lantern with his silhouette inside it, arm out, torc high — a woodcut of himself, printed in his own light.

She is inside that afterimage before it fades. No scrub, no well at range — just the walk-in her whole school is named for, wrist, cross, palm to the heart seal. And here is the measure of the man: feeling the heat gather on his breastbone, two seconds from a level bout, Dree does not break the bind. He stands in it and winds, wrist circling behind her back like a man stirring tea in a burning house. The seal slumps. The marshals call it level. His torc, nobody needs an instrument to know, is full.

“He traded a seal for a loaded winding. The gallery thought it madness. The Board’s clerk, who could count, did not.”

Score after: Halloway 2 — 2 Dree · seal, heart — and a winding held

The Board’s margin — exchange VI

wild quench: 0.427 > 0.40 (fog) → arc lives, unthreaded → forks, ±25° cone strike odds: torso 0.5 m at 3.0 m ≈ 1-in-5 per tine · log: both tines earthed her rush: 3.0 m ÷ 2.0 m/s = 1.5 s · his recovery wind: 2.0 s → bound, −0.5 s heart seal: 0.49 kJ at 260 W = 1.9 s held — he winds through it exit state: score level · his torc full · her core −1.1 K of a 1.2 K stop Verdict: she bought a point with proximity. He bought the next ten seconds with a welt over his heart. The instruments call that even. The instruments are wrong about very little.

The Last Exchange · down her own well

The sectors do not care whose hand set the condition

They break, and the marshals re-chalk, and what is left in the ring is two cold, spent people and one loaded torc. Halloway cannot afford another exchange — the thermoscopes say so even if her face will not — and so she opens her finest line, the one that ends bouts: the crown-seal well, three clean metres of gathering warmth drawn straight from her wand to the top of his shoulder. It is beautiful. The fog leans away from it. The gallery rises.

And Dree, who has been buying seconds all afternoon, spends them all at once. He does not dodge the well. He sights down it. Her line is a spine of warmed air — and warmed air is a channel, and a channel does not ask who paid for it. The quench leaps from his torc along the very corridor her art has lovingly prepared, upstream against her intent, and prints its fern across her heart seal while her heat is still en route to his collar. The crack and the slump of wax arrive together. Three. The bout.

“She fought him with fire and he answered with her own arithmetic. The Board’s margin says it plainest: the sectors do not care whose hand set the condition.”

Final: Halloway 2 — 3 Dree · fern, heart — down her own well

The Board’s margin — the last exchange

her crown line: 3.2 m sustained · spine ΔT ≈ +180 K hot-channel breakdown (Board interpolation): ≈ 0.42 MV/m his gauge: 0.427 MV/m — clears her channel by 1.7 % why no scrub? one line per wand — hers was the attack. The well was the vulnerability. first time all bout she offered three straight metres of her own warmth. She was cold, it was late, and the crown seal wins bouts. Verdict: won by the width of a gauge tolerance. From Lady Day the Board gauges tournament torcs at Λ 72 (0.384 MV/m) — the Guild curses; the Circle calls the move the Halloway Line, for she taught it to him by losing.

The Ledger · what it cost

Both paid the bill; the gallery got the laws

They took tea together at the rope, because that is the custom and because neither could have stood unassisted much longer. Sweet tea for the Ember, who had pumped a court’s worth of warmth through her own blood; honey-bread for the Needle, who had wound and re-wound a day’s eating onto his arm. The fern on her heart seal, they say, she asked the tailor to leave in the cloth.

The clerks record the bout as three–two. The schools record it otherwise: the Guild teaches Exchange the Sixth, the held winding, as the model of nerve; the Circle teaches the Last, without irony, under her name — the Halloway Line, the corridor you must never leave open behind your own fire. Rated to the same ring before the bout; rated to the same ring after. The ash, as the marshals say, rates finer than the Board — but only by one clean thought at a time.
