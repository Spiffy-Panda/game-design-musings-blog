Section A — Multiple choice12 marks · 2 each

Circle one letter per question.

A1. The law ∇·Λ = 0 tells us that [2]
  1. (a) Loom lines terminate on anima charge.
  2. (b) Loom lines form closed loops — there are no isolated Loom poles.
  3. (c) the Loom vanishes in vacuum.
  4. (d) the Loom decays with τα = 11 s.

Key · (b) Solenoidal means closed loops — Law II, printed in Maxwell’s hand. (d) confuses the field with the persistence of its charge.

A2. A duelist’s circling gesture winds the Loom primarily because [2]
  1. (a) moving anima is a current Jα, and currents spin the Loom (Law IV).
  2. (b) the changing Hearth term μθεθ ∂H/∂t dominates.
  3. (c) the rhythm of the winding chant drives the field.
  4. (d) copper in the torc attracts Λ of its own accord.

Key · (a) Both terms of Law IV are real, but at duelling tempo the displacement term is negligible: the gesture is the current. Most-picked distractor is (b) — see T. Mully’s paper, and half of §2’s.

A3. Kindle famously fails on a glacier because [2]
  1. (a) H cannot form below the freezing point.
  2. (b) Exchange I moves heat but cannot create it — the ambient reservoir is too thin.
  3. (c) ice grounds the Loom like cold iron.
  4. (d) τα shortens in the cold.

Key · (b) Exchange I is a pump, not a furnace. This is the single most examinable sentence in the course.

A4. To collapse an opponent’s standing ward, you throw at their feet a bar of [2]
  1. (a) copper.
  2. (b) silver.
  3. (c) cold iron.
  4. (d) gold.

Key · (c) Copper stores a winding; iron drains one. Mixing these up is how duelling robes catch fire. Gold does nothing, which is why it is jewellery.

A5. Frostlock’s faint violet glow is [2]
  1. (a) the ejected heat re-radiated as murmur — conservation’s receipt.
  2. (b) air ionized by the cold.
  3. (c) a reflection of the caster’s aura.
  4. (d) a myth; cold-work does not glow.

Key · (a) The pumped-out energy must go somewhere; it leaves as sector radiation. Cold light is a refund.

A6. A schooled defender can often dodge a stormneedle because [2]
  1. (a) the aiming fields travel at cθ ≈ 299 m/s and the wind-up is visible — the bolt is instant, the aim is not.
  2. (b) bolts are repelled by living tissue.
  3. (c) the discharge must follow the ground.
  4. (d) an electric field cannot cross a moving body.

Key · (a) Duels are lost in the wind-up (Vane, §7 marginalia). Section B3 makes you prove it.

Section C — Short answer16 marks · 4 each

Two to four sentences each. Cite a law where one applies.

C1. State the eleven-second rule and use it to explain why there are no enchanted swords. [4]
Anima fades outside living tissue with τ_α = 11 s (Law V), so whatever the smith puts into the blade is gone in about a breath after they let go. So a sword can’t stay magic on its own.

Model. By Law V, anima decays outside living tissue with τα ≈ 11 s — no free charge outlives a breath. A smith’s charge is gone before the blade cools, so objects cannot store magic; they can only shape a living caster’s fields. Artifacts are lenses, never batteries — the torc aims what you pour through it and does nothing on a shelf.

[1] rule stated with τ · [1] applies outside living tissue · [2] lens-vs-battery distinction Typical 2/4 Stops at “the charge fades” without the lens/battery half — the question asks why the sword is impossible, not why the charge is brief.
C2. Why are fire and frost “one discipline”? [4]
Same Exchange I well with the sign flipped — H converging pumps heat IN (fire), inverted pumps it OUT (frost). And the heat that leaves has to go somewhere, so it comes out as the violet murmur glow — the books balance either way.

Model. Exchange I makes heat flow along H; the same well geometry with inverted phase reverses the flux. Kindle and Frostlock differ by a sign, not a subject — and conservation holds in both directions, frost’s exported heat leaving as murmur.

[2] sign inversion of the same well · [2] conservation both ways
C3. Every Loomshell has at least two weak points. Name them, and the theorem that guarantees them. [4]
It’s the hairy-ball thing — you can’t comb a sphere flat, so there are two bald whorls (top of the head + under the feet). Duelists aim at the crown one.

Model. The polar whorls — index defects where the combed tangent field must vanish. Poincaré–Hopf: any tangent vector field on a sphere has zeroes of total index 2, so no combing removes them; every shell carries two, and the crown whorl is the classic target.

[1] names the whorls/defects · [2] Poincaré–Hopf (accept “hairy-ball theorem”) · [1] tactical consequence Note “The hairy-ball thing” earns the idea mark but not the theorem mark. Names matter; they are how you find the literature before the literature finds you.
C4. A first-year claims: “the chant pours energy into the bolt.” Give the correct account of what words do, and where the energy actually comes from. [4]
Words don’t add any energy — the energy is metabolic (you eat for your bolts). Pretty sure the words are just tradition.

Model. No syllable couples to the fields. Words are the clock: the caster phase-locks the drive to their own pulse, and a chant is a metronome you can carry into fear — a gagged caster still casts, only raggedly. The energy ledger is metabolic, spent through the anima the gesture and focus deploy (gesture writes the current, focus sets the boundary, word keeps time).

[2] words = timing/phase-lock, not energy · [2] metabolic ledger via anima Typical 2/4 “Words do nothing” is half the story — they do nothing to the fields, and something essential to the caster. Tradition that survives four centuries of duelling is rarely decoration.
