How judging works
  • Windows: ±30 ms perfect · ±70 ms good · ±120 ms off · beyond = stray. Unplayed steps = miss.
  • Bias is the signed mean of your hit errors: positive = dragging (late), negative = rushing. It's the number to coach — see MDC.4.
  • Only the lanes in the current grid are judged; everything else is free.
  • E-kit through an interface? Set the input latency slider until on-the-click playing reads bias ≈ 0.

The library

Everything runs in the page: hits are processed locally and never leave the tab (the latency setting and your pad map are the only things kept, in this browser's storage). Web MIDI needs Chromium or Firefox and a permission grant; pads default to the General MIDI drum map (kick 35/36, snare 37/38/40, hats 42/44/46, toms 41–50, cymbals 49–59) — if your kit speaks something else (hi-hats are notorious), the monitor line shows the raw note and Map pads rebinds it by ear: click the lane, hit the drum. Sounds are synthesized on the spot; the first click anywhere wakes the audio engine. Part of the MIDI Drum Coach musing — the director's rules R1–R5 are the point (MDC.2).
