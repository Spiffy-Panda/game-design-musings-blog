# VIGNETTE-HANDOFF.md — LoMa everyday-life vignettes (reusable chat handoff)

**You are receiving this in a fresh chat.** Your job: propose short abstracts /
writing-prompts for **vignettes — stories of the everyday lives of ordinary people in the
world of Logical Magic (LoMa)** — wait for Panda to pick, then write the chosen ones. This
document is your complete briefing: the system, the canon, the numbers, the guardrails,
and the resource list. It is self-contained; repo access is optional (pointers at the end).

Division of labor: **this doc explains the world; you invent the prompts and the prose.**
Do not re-derive or alter the system — inhabit it.

---

## 1 · The world in one page

Magic is real, and it is **mathematical logic taken literally**. The world is a model — a
vast ledger of atomic facts (the **Record**) over a domain of things, floored by a short
list of protected axioms (the **Bedrock**: things persist; the dead stay dead; no paradox
engines). A **spell** — formally a **plea** — is a sentence in a formal language (the
**Tongue**) that the world does not yet satisfy.

Casting is a four-step transaction: **Scope** (fix the domain — you can only quantify over
what you can name or perceive), **Say** (utter the sentence; ill-formed strings simply
aren't spells), **Warrant** (supply a proof that your sentence is consistent with the
Bedrock — paid in **strokes**, one per inference step; a drilled caster manages ~4 strokes
a second aloud), **Settle** (the world revises itself to a nearest model of your sentence —
paid in **flips**, one per atomic fact changed, drawn from the local pool of **grace**).

Two ledgers, two payers: strokes are *your* labor; flips are *the world's*. You do not pay
for how much burns — you pay for how hard it is to lawfully say "all of it."

**The Miser's Law:** among all ways of making your sentence true, the world picks one that
flips the fewest facts — and among those, it owes you no kindness. Every monkey's-paw story
is this one law. Safety is purchased clause by clause, each rider at a stroke price.
"A wish is exactly as safe as it is long."

## 2 · The rules as lived experience

1. **∃ is cheap and dangerous; ∀ is honest and expensive.** Wishes hand the world a blank;
   wards make promises over everything in scope. Casters climb "circles" = quantifier
   alternation depth. Cantrips (light *the* candle) are one stroke, one flip.
2. **Structure is wealth.** A ∀ over a constructible domain (a chain, a wall, a
   grid-scoped sphere) can be warranted by *induction* — 13 strokes instead of hundreds of
   thousands. A crowd has no constructor: brute strokes, or pay to impose an order first.
   Induction never reduces *flips* — the world's bill stands.
3. **Grace is local and slow.** A quiet vale accrues ~10,000 flips of grace a day. Spend a
   region dry and it goes **stiff** — lawful pleas fail to settle until the tide refills.
   Hence sacred ground, grace-thrift customs, and why big magic is a strategic decision.
4. **Preparation is the combat economy.** A **scroll** is a warrant paid for last night —
   strokes spent at inscription, spent in one breath at need, single-use. (Yes: spell
   slots, honestly derived.) A **grimoire** is a lemma cache — knowing more lemmas makes
   the same spell literally cheaper. Skill = proof brevity.
5. **Opposed magic is cross-examination.** Evaluating a sentence is a two-seat game:
   ∃-moves are the caster's, ∀-moves belong to the **falsifier**. An enemy may take the
   falsifier's seat and play your ∀'s viciously — name the one blade your parry-proof
   forgot. Duels, courtrooms, and customs inspections all run on this.
6. **Wards are theories; breaches are proofs.** A ward is a standing writ on an
   anchor-stone that refuses violating settlements — and admits any petition it can
   *derive*. Three lawful breach families: predicate lawyering (satisfy the writ's
   definitions de jure), the unmeant model (enter by an interpretation the inscriber never
   fixed — the old well that predates the survey), and explosion (find the day the writ
   contradicts itself; a checker holding ⊥ countersigns anything). Premium anchors are cut
   **paraconsistent** ("a stone that can hold two truths without going mad") at 4× price.
7. **Rituals are transactions.** Multi-step magic is poured through **vessels** (monads):
   step ▷ step ▷ step, then **bottle** — the settlement files once, at the end, or not at
   all. A **crack** anywhere exits with the Record unchanged: no half-mended arm. Raw
   unvesseled chains are how magical accidents happen. The **Vessel of Ash** logs every
   pour as ineffaceable residue — forensic thaumaturgy reads ash; **silent casting**
   (pouring over the trivial monoid) is lawful mathematics and outlawed everywhere,
   detectable only by the shape of the hole it leaves.
8. **Enchantment is vocabulary.** Second-order casters **charter** new predicates at the
   Mint (fee = the defining formula's length; upkeep = a daily grace tithe). Stop feeding
   a charter and the word fades — that is what "the enchantment wore off" means. **True
   names**: a finite soul is ~10⁶ facts (a lifetime's transcription — heirloom
   name-scrolls, capital theft); infinite things (rivers, the sky) cannot be pinned in
   first order at all — which is exactly why hedge-wizards cannot command rivers and
   archmagi can.
9. **The Limits are real theorems and cannot be patched.** The Silent Truths (Gödel: true
   miracles with no warrant at any price), No Mirror (Tarski: no scry shows Truth entire),
   No Bootstraps (Löb: faith-casting buys nothing), No Assay (Rice: cursed rings pass
   inspection — you can only *run* a strange charter), the Patient Siege (compactness:
   wards fall to one-innocent-day-at-a-time), the Unmeant (Löwenheim–Skolem: every
   infinite treaty has readings nobody meant — **the fae live there**, keeping the letter
   of every ward in interpretations no signatory fixed).
10. **Failure has manners.** A collapsed plea forfeits its strokes and flips nothing. A
    cracked rite leaves the world as it was. The dangerous failures are the *successful*
    ones — the under-claused wish, the writ with a quarter-day in it.

## 3 · Glossary (in-world register)

plea (spell) · the Tongue (the formal language) · the Record (all atomic facts) · the
Bedrock (protected axioms) · warrant (the consistency proof; also the scroll it's written
on) · settlement (the world's revision) · strokes (proof-labor unit) · flips (facts
changed) · grace (the local flip budget) · stiff (grace-dry, magic-dead) · the Miser's Law
(minimal settlement, no kindness) · rider (a wish-safety clause, priced in strokes) ·
falsifier's seat (playing the ∀'s in someone's plea) · vessel / pour (▷) / bottle / crack
(monad / bind / return / failure) · the Three Duties (the monad laws; "grouping is free,
order is fate") · Vessel of Ash (Writer; residue) · silent casting (trivial-monoid
pouring; outlawed) · Vessel of Many (scrying; all futures, unindexed) · the Sealed Vessel
(the world itself; nobody holds `run`) · charter / the Mint / tithe (second-order
predicate, its office, its upkeep) · true name (categorical description) · the Unmeant
(nonstandard-model creatures; fae) · the Silent Truths (Gödel's leftovers) · prepared
warrant / scroll (Vancian slot) · circle (quantifier-alternation rank).

## 5 · Canon so far (people, places, texts, incidents — all fictional)

- **Vell College** — a college of warrants; **Corvane**, Reader in Warrants (voice of the
  pitch's marginalia; presides over LOMA 101). **M. Sedge** — a LOMA 101 student whose
  imperfect problem-set answers are canon (mixes up the Second and Third Duties).
- **The Brindle Casebook** — the standard casebook of wishes gone wrong ("a horse that
  cannot lose" → a horse that cannot race).
- **Bellhall** — the manor whose ward held three axioms and a quarter-day contradiction;
  breached for 8 strokes by the sapper **Corb Fen**; the repaired writ is paraconsistent.
- **The Assize of Bells** — Crown v. Fen, the trial-as-duel that followed: **Justice
  Ambry** (the Bench), **Serjeant Marrow** (Crown), **A. Quill** (defense), the **Clerks
  of the Ledger** (auditors). Established at law: the **Rule of Sound Warrant** (courts
  accept no derivation that passes through ⊥ — the law runs relevance logic even where
  stones don't); acquitted of trespass ("no writ, no wall"), convicted of silent casting
  by the ash-gap his reconnaissance left.
- **The Treaty of the Unmeant** — the standing accord with the fae; its preamble: "We do
  not fear the mage who breaks the law. We fear the one who reads it to the end."
- Professions in good standing: ward-wrights, chirurgeon-logicians (vessel-certified
  healers), scroll-scribes, Mint clerks and the Mint-Warden, forensic ash-readers, seers'
  guild (they argue about ordering functions), treaty lawyers, drillmasters.

## 6 · Texture inventory (raw material — NOT prompts; the prompts are your job)

Everyday consequences a vignette can stand on: the scroll economy (warrants inscribed at
night, spent in a breath); wish-lawyering as a paid trade (riders drafted per stroke; the
Penny Gilding as ordinary commerce); enchantment tithes as recurring household bills — and
what fades first when a family economizes; stiff regions after big castings (grace drought
as weather); sacred ground as managed grace-reserve; ash-audits (tax inspections, crime
scenes, insurance); silent-casting as the signature crime of the discreet; name-scrolls as
heirlooms and dowries; the well-known peril of under-clased wishes among the poor (riders
cost strokes the unschooled don't have — safety is regressive); apprentices drilled on
stroke-rate and lemma memorization; treaty-clause fae encounters (the letter kept, the
meaning not); customs inspections as falsifier-seat games; the impossibility of detecting
a cursed ring (No Assay) and the provenance-paper trade that fills the gap; coarse-grain
magic's aliasing (the singed rim) as a class marker — fine grain is for those who can
drain a vale.

## 7 · Guardrails

- **Vignettes are ordinary days.** Laundry, tolls, apprenticeships, small commerce, minor
  crimes. The magic stays small and *priced* — when someone casts, the strokes/flips are
  right (or knowingly rounded by the narrator). No archmagi saving the world.
- **The math must stay honest.** Use the system as given; don't invent new theorems or
  new constants. If a story needs a number this doc lacks, keep it qualitative or flag it
  as a proposed addition for Panda to ratify.
- **All names fictional** (people, places, institutions). No real-world persons. If the
  text returns to the repo it passes a public-surface gate: no private names or paths.
- **Tone reference:** the pitch page's marginalia — dry, legalistic warmth; proverbs that
  turn out to be theorems. The world is matter-of-fact about magic the way we are about
  electricity.
- **Handles:** number finished vignettes `LVIG.1`, `LVIG.2`, … (LoMa vignettes — distinct
  from Space Feudal's `VIG.n` series). Keep each under ~900 words unless asked otherwise.
- **Workflow:** propose 6–10 one-line abstracts first (title + a sentence). Wait for
  Panda's picks. Then write. Return Markdown, one `## LVIG.n — Title` per vignette, with a
  one-line abstract above each body (they may later become a companion page of the
  logical-magic musing, like Space Feudal's vignette page).
