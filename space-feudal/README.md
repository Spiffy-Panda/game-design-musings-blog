# Space Feudal

A game-design musing: the cockpit is rented from the genre (X series, Elite: Dangerous,
No Man's Sky); the design work is the economic layer above it. Rebuild the six economic
problems feudalism actually solved — authority lag, money that fails at distance, raids
faster than armies, bulk that can't travel, contracts nobody can enforce, offices that
die with their holders — with invented physics (fixed FTL lanes; **chrism**, a jump fuel
with a 90-day half-life; crewed-only harvests; no signal faster than a hull), and let
hereditary lords condense back out of the simulation.

## Reading order

1. **`index.html` — the brief.** The wager, the six pillar-problems, the invented kit,
   the worked numbers ("the tyranny of the fade"), the company→fief emergence ladder,
   what the pilot feels, and the four "gunpowder moments" that can end the age.
2. **`ledger.html` — the Ledger of Correspondences.** How the old and the to-be line
   up: 25 rows (`SF.1`–`SF.25`), each pairing a medieval institution with its
   Weave-born twin and the mechanic that carries it.
3. **`sieges.html` — The Long Patience.** Siegecraft, honestly: a closed-loop keep can
   feed itself for generations, so a siege is two clocks — the garrison's decaying
   industry against the besieger's melting logistics. Six endings (`SIEGE.1`–`SIEGE.6`)
   and the Harrow system map (scale fudged on purpose; distances and transit times
   marked true).
4. **`loom.html` — The Loom.** Emergent consequences chased and tuned: six pulled
   threads (`LOOM.1`–`LOOM.6`, starting from the drone home-shell no mobile fleet can
   match), each checked by counterweights found in canon and ended on a feel dial.

All pages are self-contained HTML — open them straight from disk, no build, no
dependencies; light and dark themes included. Published on the site via the repo's
verbatim-copy build (`build-musing.py`).
